Old Testament #15

Jacob and Esau

Scripture

Gen. 25-27

Summary

Chapt. 25: Isaac prayed that God would give he and Rebekah children. God gave them twins, Jacob and Esau. Even before they were born, God said that the older son, Esau, would serve the younger son, Jacob (Gen. 25:23). One day, Jacob tricked Esau into giving him the birthright (special rights for the 1st born son) for a bowl of soup. Esau chose to have the soup.

Chapt. 26: God repeated His promises to Isaac (v.2-4). Isaac went to Gerar because of a famine and told the people that Rebekah was his sister because he was afraid that they would kill him. The king of Gerar found out the truth and said that no one should harm them. God blessed Isaac in the land and people envied him (v. 14). God told Isaac that He was with him and not to fear. Isaac worshiped God (v. 23-25) .

Chapt. 27: Later, when their father Isaac was old, he wanted to bless Esau (the firstborn) in a special way. Esau left to hunt special food for his father. Rebekah, told Jacob to dress up like Esau and he deceived his father into giving him the special blessing. Esau was very angry with Jacob when he found out what happened.
